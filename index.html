Biography

Tri-Nhu Do was born in Da Nang, Vietnam. He earned a B.S. degree in electronics and telecommunications engineering from the Posts and Telecommunications Institute of Technology (PTIT), Vietnam, in 2012. He earned a Ph.D. degree and an M.S. degree in electronics and computer engineering from Hongik University, South Korea, in 2018 and 2015, respectively. He was a Teaching Associate with the Department of Software and Communications Engineering at Hongik University in 2017 to 2018. He was a Postdoctoral Research Associate with the Department of Computer Science at the University of Texas at Dallas in 2019.

Research Interest

My research involves in various theoretical and practical aspects of wireless communications. The research blends knowledge and tools from communication and signal processing theory, probability and statistics theory, and optimization theory. The current research topics of interest are:

  • Wireless communications
  • Cooperative transmissions
  • Multiple-input multiple-output (MIMO)

and the past research topics of interest are:

  • Non-orthogonal multiple access (NOMA)
  • Simultaneous wireless information and power transfer (SWIPT)
  • Physical layer security (PHY-security)
  • Cognitive radio
