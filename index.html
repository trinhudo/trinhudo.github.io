Biography

Tri-Nhu Do was born in Da Nang, Vietnam. He is now a Research Associate in the department of Computer Science at the University of Texas at Dallas, TX, USA. He received the Ph.D. and M.S. degrees in electronics and computer engineering from Hongik University, South Korea, in 2018 and 2015, respectively. He also earned the B.S. degree in electronics and telecommunications engineering from the Posts and Telecommunications Institute of Technology (PTIT), Vietnam, in 2012. He was also a Teaching Associate with the Department of Software and Communications Engineering at Hongik University from Mar. 2017 to Jun. 2018.

Research Interest

My research involves in various theoretical and practical aspects of wireless communications. The research blends knowledge and tools from communication and signal processing theory, probability and statistics theory, and optimization theory. The current research topics of interest are:

  • Wireless communications
  • Cooperative transmissions
  • Multiple-input multiple-output (MIMO)

and the past research topics of interest are:

  • Non-orthogonal multiple access (NOMA)
  • Simultaneous wireless information and power transfer (SWIPT)
  • Physical layer security (PHY-security)
  • Cognitive radio
