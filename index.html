Biography

Tri-Nhu (Neil) Do was born in Da Nang, Vietnam. He received the B.S. degree in electronics and telecommunications engineering from the Posts and Telecommunications Institute of Technology (PTIT), Vietnam, in 2012, and the M.S. and Ph.D. degrees in electronics and computer engineering from Hongik University, South Korea, in 2015 and 2018, respectively. He was also a Teaching Associate with the Department of Software and Communications Engineering at Hongik University from Mar. 2017 to Jun. 2018.

Research Interest

My research involves in various theoretical and practical aspects of wireless communications. The research blends knowledge and tools from communication and signal processing theory, probability and statistics theory, and optimization theory. The current research topics of interest are:

  • Applications of machine learning in wireless communication
  • Wireless communications
  • Cooperative transmissions
  • Multiple-input multiple-output (MIMO) and Massive MIMO
  • Millimeter wave (mmWave)

and the past research topics of interest are:

  • Non-orthogonal multiple access (NOMA)
  • Simultaneous wireless information and power transfer (SWIPT)
  • Physical layer security (PHY-security)
  • Cognitive radio
